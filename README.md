I am a fourth-year undergraduate student at the University of California, Santa Cruz. I also work as a researcher at [Professor Jeffrey Flanigan](https://jflanigan.github.io/)'s JLab.

My interests lie in sample-efficient learning, including [reinforcement learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.06339v1) (learning by trial and error), [meta-learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/2004.05439v1) (learning to learn), [few-shot learning](https://arxiv.org/abs/1904.05046v3) (learning with a small amount of training data), and [out-of-distribution generalization](http://www.gatsby.ucl.ac.uk/~balaji/berkeley-talk-balaji.pdf) (learning to generalize to unseen examples).  I aim to develop universal methods to allow agents to learn quickly and efficiently and to be able to generalize to out-of-distribution examples or tasks at test time. My life goal is to build intelligent agents that can discover and improve themselves.
